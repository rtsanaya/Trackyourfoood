Let's talk about Street Food?

Street food refers to ready-to-eat food and beverages sold by vendors or hawkers in public spaces, typically on streets, markets, or festivals. These foods are often sold from food stalls and designed to be convenient, affordable, and quick to prepare.

TOP 10 STREET FOOD you should try!

1. Pempek

A type of Indonesian fishcake, from the city of Palembang in South Sumatra. It is made from a mixture of fish (usually tuna or tilapia), flour, and various seasonings, and is often served with a spicy and sour peanut-based sauce called 'kuah cuko'.

3. Martabak Manis

Martabak manis is a popular Indonesian dessert that is similar to a pancake. It is made from a batter that is typically made from flour, eggs, and sugar, and is cooked on a hot griddle. The pancake is then filled with a variety of sweet toppings, such as sweetened condensed milk, jackfruit, or red bean paste.

4. Roti Bakar

Roti bakar is a type of bread that is popular in Indonesia and other parts of Southeast Asia. It is a type of flatbread that is made from a simple dough of flour, water, and salt, and is baked until it is crispy and golden brown.

5. Es Teler

Es teler is a popular Indonesian drink that is made from young coconut (teler) juice. It is a refreshing and sweet drink that is often served over ice and is flavored with a variety of sweeteners, such as sugar or condensed milk. Es teler is a popular drink in Indonesia and is often enjoyed as a refreshing and cooling beverage on hot summer days.

6. Kue Cubit

Kue cubit a type of traditional Indonesian cake that is made from a batter of flour, eggs, and sugar. It is typically baked in a square or rectangular shape and is often served as a snack or dessert. Kue cubit is a popular and well-loved cake in Indonesia and is often enjoyed with a cup of tea or coffee.

7. Cilok

Cilok is a popular Indonesian snack that is similar to a potato chip. It is made from thinly sliced potatoes that are fried until they are crispy and golden brown. Cilok is often seasoned with a variety of spices and herbs, such as garlic, ginger, and chili powder, and is typically served with a side of mayonnaise or other dipping sauce.

8. Seblak

Seblak is a popular Indonesian snack that is made from a mixture of ground meat (usually beef or chicken), flour, and various seasonings. The mixture is then formed into small balls or patties and is deep-fried until it is crispy and golden brown.

It is a type of dumpling that is made from a thin wrapper of dough and is typically filled with a variety of ingredients, such as fish, meat, or vegetables.
